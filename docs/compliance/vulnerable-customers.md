## 1. Our commitment

Bolster exists specifically to support people in financial difficulty. Our entire recipient user base meets the FCA's definition of vulnerability under FG21/1 — financial difficulty is a recognised driver of vulnerability.

This policy operationalises our commitment to treating all customers fairly, with particular care for those whose circumstances make them most susceptible to harm.

---

## 2. Who is vulnerable?

Under FCA FG21/1, a vulnerable customer is one who, due to personal circumstances, is especially susceptible to harm, particularly when a firm is not acting with appropriate care.

**Characteristics of vulnerability we expect to encounter**:

| Driver | Examples in Bolster context |
|--------|----------------------------|
| **Health** | Mental health conditions (anxiety, depression common in financial hardship); chronic illness affecting ability to work |
| **Life events** | Redundancy, bereavement, relationship breakdown, domestic abuse |
| **Resilience** | Low financial resilience; less than £100 in savings; no financial buffer |
| **Capability** | Low financial literacy; English as second language; digital exclusion |

All recipients are experiencing financial difficulty by definition. We treat every recipient interaction as a potential vulnerability scenario.

---

## 3. How we identify vulnerability

### At onboarding
- KYC flow includes optional disclosure of support needs
- Plain language throughout — no jargon
- Alternative support pathways clearly signposted (StepChange, CAB)

### During use
- Monitoring for signs of coercive control (see Section 6)
- Customer support trained to recognise distress indicators
- Multiple contact methods available (not just in-app chat)

### Self-disclosure
- Recipients can flag support needs at any time via settings
- Flags are recorded and actioned within 24 hours

---

## 4. How we respond to vulnerability

**Adjustments we can make**:
- Extended timeframes for decisions
- Simplified communications
- Alternative contact methods (phone where available)
- Referral to specialist debt advice (StepChange, Citizens Advice, National Debtline)
- Breathing space — pausing invite activity while customer stabilises
- Signposting to mental health support (Mind, Samaritans)

**What we never do**:
- Rush a vulnerable customer through a process
- Use language that adds to shame or stigma
- Allow collection-style pressure from any direction
- Share recipient information without explicit consent

---

## 5. Product design standards

Every Bolster product decision is evaluated against these standards:

1. **Language**: Plain English throughout. No financial jargon. Tested with plain language tool.
2. **Dignity**: No language that implies the recipient has failed. Focus on community support, not personal failure.
3. **Control**: Recipient always controls what is shared and with whom. Privacy settings default to maximum privacy.
4. **Exit**: Recipient can revoke any invite immediately, at any time, with no questions asked.
5. **Support pathways**: Every page where a recipient might be distressed includes a link to free debt advice.
6. **Error messages**: Payment failures and KYC declines use neutral, non-shaming language.

---

## 6. Coercive control safeguards

Bolster's design could theoretically be exploited in coercive control situations — a controlling partner forcing someone to create an invite and directing payments to a covertly controlled creditor account.

**Mitigations built into the product**:
- Recipient-only consent: only the verified account holder can create invites
- Instant revocation: any invite can be cancelled immediately
- CoP verification on creditor accounts prevents routing to unknown accounts
- No visibility of contributor's bank details by recipient
- Support team trained on economic abuse indicators
- Referral to Surviving Economic Abuse (SEA) for affected users

We work with [Debt charity partner] to review our coercive control policies annually.

## 8. Staff training

All customer-facing staff complete vulnerable customer training covering:
- Identifying vulnerability indicators
- Adjusting communication style
- Making referrals appropriately
- Recognising economic abuse
- Self-care for staff handling distressing interactions

Training is completed on joining and refreshed annually.

---

## 9. Monitoring and outcomes

We track the following metrics to monitor outcomes for vulnerable customers:
- Percentage of recipients who complete KYC successfully
- Invite link conversion rate (invites created → payments received)
- Payment failure rate and reasons
- Support contact frequency and resolution time
- Referral rate to external debt advice

Reported quarterly to Board. Any deterioration in outcomes triggers a product review.

## 10. Consumer Duty alignment

This policy supports delivery of all four Consumer Duty outcomes (PRIN 2A):

| Outcome | How Bolster delivers |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Products and services** | Designed specifically for people in financial hardship; not repurposed from other use cases |
| **Fair value** | 1.5% fee charged to contributor, not recipient; no cost to person in hardship |
| **Consumer understanding** | Plain language; clear explanation of what happens to money |
| **Consumer support** | Multiple support channels; referrals; trained staff |

Consumer Duty Assessment maintained separately and reviewed annually.
